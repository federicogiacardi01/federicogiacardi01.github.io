I'm a computer science student interested in applying computational techniques to solve science and engineering problems. From a very young age, my life has been driven by the desire to build complex systems that could impact everyone's life. Computer science has therefore been a natural career path for me, given its ability to blend engineering and math. After earning a bachelor's degree that fueled my desire to learn and provided a new perspective on complex problems, I decided to pursue a master's degree in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Turin. My bachelor's thesis exposed me to scientific research and resulted in a fellowship on the application of high-performance and distributed computing to large graph processing and autonomous team formation. I completed my Master's thesis after a 2-months internship at INRA Saclay, where I worked on predicting cognitive processes from functional MRI data using machine learning. My research interests now sits at the intersection of representation learning, domain adaptation, and computational neuroscience, with a particular focus on building robust, generalizable models of cognition from neuroimaging data. I am currently pursuing a PhD position to extend this work towards a foundation model for human cognition trained on large, harmonized multi-site fMRI datasets.
